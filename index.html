Kepler-452b

Kepler-452b, located about 1,400 light-years away in the constellation Cygnus, is often called "Earth’s cousin" due to its similar size and rocky nature. Orbiting its star, Kepler-452, in the habitable zone, it may have conditions suitable for liquid water.

Gliese 581g

Gliese 581g is an exoplanet located approximately 20.3 light-years away in the constellation Libra. It orbits within the habitable zone of its red dwarf star, Gliese 581.

3D view
TRAPPIST-1d

TRAPPIST-1d is an exoplanet located about 39 light-years away in the constellation Aquarius. It orbits within the habitable zone of its star, where conditions might allow for liquid water.

3D view
LHS 1140 b

LHS 1140 b is an exoplanet located approximately 40 light-years away in the constellation of Eridanus. It orbits its host star, LHS 1140, which is a red dwarf.

3D view
K2-18b

K2-18b is an exoplanet located approximately 124 light-years away in the constellation Leo. It orbits within the habitable zone of its host star.
